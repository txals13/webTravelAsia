Discover Bali

A Perfect Blend of Nature, Culture, and Adventure

Transportation to Bali

Bali is accessible by air through Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS), also known as Denpasar International Airport. The airport is located in southern Bali, close to popular tourist destinations such as Kuta, Seminyak, and Nusa Dua. Domestic flights from other Indonesian cities are frequent, and taxis and private transfers are available for travel within the island.

Schedules for Popular Tours

Bali offers a variety of tours that showcase its rich culture, scenic landscapes, and adventure activities. Here are some popular tours:

  • Ubud Art and Culture Tour: Daily departures at 9:00 AM. Explore Ubud’s famous rice terraces, temples, and art markets, and visit the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary.
  • Mount Batur Sunrise Trek: Daily at 3:00 AM. Trek up Mount Batur for a breathtaking sunrise view and enjoy breakfast with a panoramic landscape.
  • Nusa Penida Island Tour: Available daily, departing at 7:00 AM. Visit the stunning beaches of Nusa Penida, including Kelingking Beach and Broken Beach.
  • Tanah Lot Temple and Sunset Tour: Departs at 2:00 PM daily. Discover the iconic Tanah Lot Temple perched on a rock formation and witness a magical sunset.
  • Bali White Water Rafting Adventure: Tours available at 9:00 AM and 1:00 PM. Enjoy an adrenaline-filled rafting experience on the Ayung River.

Interesting Places to Visit

Bali is known for its natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and pristine beaches. Here are some must-visit places:

  • Ubud: Known as Bali's cultural heart, Ubud offers lush rice terraces, traditional Balinese art, and serene temples. Don’t miss the Sacred Monkey Forest and Tegallalang Rice Terraces.
  • Mount Batur: An active volcano that offers an exhilarating sunrise trekking experience. Visitors are rewarded with stunning views over the island.
  • Nusa Penida: A small island southeast of Bali, known for its dramatic cliffs, crystal-clear waters, and secluded beaches such as Kelingking and Angel’s Billabong.
  • Tanah Lot Temple: One of Bali’s most famous sea temples, perched on a rocky outcrop in the ocean. It is a spectacular spot to witness a Balinese sunset.
  • Seminyak Beach: A lively beach resort area offering luxury hotels, high-end restaurants, beach clubs, and beautiful sunset views.
  • Tirta Empul Temple: Located near Ubud, this Hindu Balinese water temple is famous for its holy spring, where locals and visitors alike come to purify themselves in the sacred water.

Travel Tips

- Best Time to Visit: The best time to visit Bali is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The rainy season lasts from November to April, but rain showers are often brief and followed by sunshine.
- Currency: The local currency is the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). ATMs are widely available, and credit cards are accepted in most hotels, restaurants, and larger shops.
- Getting Around: Taxis, scooters, and private drivers are common means of transportation in Bali. Ride-hailing apps like Gojek and Grab are also available for convenient travel.
