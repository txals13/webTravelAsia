Discover Indonesia

Islands, Volcanoes, and Pristine Beaches Await

Transportation to Indonesia

Indonesia is served by several international airports, with the busiest being:

  • Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) in Bali
  • Soekarno-Hatta International Airport (CGK) in Jakarta
  • Juanda International Airport (SUB) in Surabaya
Domestic flights are the best way to explore Indonesia’s many islands, with budget airlines such as Lion Air, Garuda Indonesia, and AirAsia connecting popular destinations. Ferries and fast boats are available for shorter inter-island travel.

Schedules for Popular Tours

Indonesia offers a variety of tours that showcase its diverse landscape and rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Bali Temple and Rice Terrace Tour: Daily at 9:00 AM. Explore the famous temples of Uluwatu and Tanah Lot, and enjoy the scenic rice terraces of Tegalalang.
  • Mount Bromo Sunrise Trek: Departs from Surabaya at 2:00 AM. Experience a breathtaking sunrise over one of Indonesia’s most famous volcanoes.
  • Komodo Island and Pink Beach Tour: Available daily from Labuan Bajo. Visit the Komodo dragons and enjoy snorkeling at Pink Beach, known for its unique pink-hued sand.
  • Ubud Art and Culture Tour: Departs daily at 9:30 AM from Bali. Visit Ubud’s art galleries, local markets, and the sacred Monkey Forest.
  • Gili Islands Snorkeling Trip: Daily departures from Lombok. Explore the vibrant coral reefs and marine life surrounding the Gili Islands.

Interesting Places to Visit

Indonesia is an archipelago of over 17,000 islands, offering everything from beaches to jungles to volcanoes. Here are some must-visit destinations:

  • Bali: Indonesia’s most popular island destination, Bali is known for its stunning beaches, lush rice terraces, and vibrant culture. Don’t miss Uluwatu Temple, Tanah Lot, and the lively town of Ubud.
  • Komodo National Park: Home to the famous Komodo dragons, this UNESCO World Heritage site offers dramatic landscapes, snorkeling, and diving around its coral reefs.
  • Mount Bromo: One of Indonesia’s most iconic volcanoes, Mount Bromo offers stunning sunrise views and an otherworldly landscape. It’s part of the Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park in East Java.
  • Yogyakarta and Borobudur: Yogyakarta is the cultural heart of Java, known for traditional art, dance, and music. Nearby, visit Borobudur, the world’s largest Buddhist temple, and the stunning Prambanan Hindu temple complex.
  • Gili Islands: A group of three small islands off the coast of Lombok, the Gili Islands are perfect for snorkeling, diving, and enjoying a laid-back atmosphere.
  • Sumatra: Explore Sumatra’s natural beauty, including Lake Toba (the world’s largest volcanic lake), orangutan sanctuaries in Bukit Lawang, and the waterfalls of Berastagi.

Travel Tips

- Best Time to Visit: The best time to visit Indonesia is during the dry season, from May to September. The wet season runs from October to April, though some regions experience less rainfall than others.
- Currency: The local currency is the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). ATMs are widely available, and credit cards are accepted in major cities and tourist areas.
- Getting Around: Domestic flights are the most convenient way to travel between Indonesia’s islands. For shorter distances, ferries and fast boats connect popular islands, while taxis and motorbike rentals are common on land.
