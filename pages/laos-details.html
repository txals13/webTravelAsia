Discover Laos

The Hidden Gem of Southeast Asia

Transportation to Laos

Laos is reachable by air and land. Key international airports include:

  • Wattay International Airport (VTE) in Vientiane
  • Luang Prabang International Airport (LPQ) in Luang Prabang
You can also enter Laos overland from neighboring countries, including Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia, and China, via various border crossings.

Schedules for Popular Tours

Some of the most popular tours in Laos offer both cultural immersion and natural beauty. Here are a few options:

  • Luang Prabang City and Temple Tour: Departs daily at 9:00 AM and 2:00 PM. Explore the UNESCO World Heritage city with its ancient temples and vibrant local markets.
  • Kuang Si Waterfalls Trip: Daily departures at 10:00 AM from Luang Prabang. Includes a swim at the waterfalls and a visit to the bear sanctuary.
  • Mekong River Sunset Cruise: Departs daily at 5:00 PM from Vientiane. A relaxing way to enjoy the beauty of the Mekong River at sunset.
  • Vang Vieng Cave and Kayaking Tour: Available daily at 8:30 AM. Includes caving, kayaking, and stunning views of limestone mountains.
  • Buddha Park Tour: Daily at 9:00 AM from Vientiane. Visit the famous sculpture park with over 200 Hindu and Buddhist statues.

Interesting Places to Visit

Laos is home to stunning natural landscapes and rich cultural history. Here are some must-visit places:

  • Luang Prabang: A UNESCO World Heritage city known for its well-preserved temples, colonial architecture, and the beautiful Mount Phousi viewpoint. Don’t miss the alms-giving ceremony with local monks in the early morning.
  • Kuang Si Waterfalls: A stunning multi-tiered waterfall with turquoise blue pools, located just outside of Luang Prabang. It’s a perfect spot for swimming and picnicking.
  • Vang Vieng: A small town surrounded by limestone mountains, caves, and the Nam Song River. Famous for adventure activities like tubing, kayaking, and rock climbing.
  • Buddha Park: Also known as Xieng Khuan, this sculpture park near Vientiane features over 200 Buddhist and Hindu statues, offering a surreal experience.
  • Plain of Jars: Located in central Laos, this archaeological site features thousands of ancient stone jars scattered across the landscape. Its origins remain a mystery.
  • Vat Phou: A ruined Khmer temple complex in southern Laos, designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site, offering stunning views over the Mekong River.

Travel Tips

- Best Time to Visit: The best time to visit Laos is from November to February when the weather is cooler and drier. The wet season runs from May to October, which brings lush greenery but also occasional travel disruptions.
- Currency: The local currency is the Lao Kip (LAK). Cash is preferred in many areas, though major hotels and some restaurants accept credit cards.
- Getting Around: Laos is a landlocked country, so getting around is typically done via bus, minivan, or riverboat. Tuk-tuks are common for short distances in cities.
