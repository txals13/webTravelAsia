Discover the Philippines

An Archipelago of Over 7,000 Islands Awaits

Transportation to the Philippines

The Philippines is easily accessible by air, with the main international airports located in:

  • Ninoy Aquino International Airport (MNL) in Manila
  • Mactan-Cebu International Airport (CEB) in Cebu
  • Clark International Airport (CRK) in Pampanga
Domestic flights connect the islands, with major carriers like Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, and AirAsia serving popular destinations. Ferries and fast boats are available for inter-island travel.

Schedules for Popular Tours

The Philippines offers a range of tours that highlight its beautiful beaches, islands, and rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the most popular tours:

  • Island Hopping in Palawan: Daily at 9:00 AM. Explore the islands of El Nido or Coron, featuring crystal-clear waters, hidden lagoons, and vibrant coral reefs.
  • Taal Volcano Trek: Departs from Manila daily at 6:00 AM. Trek to the summit of this active volcano on an island in the middle of Taal Lake.
  • Whale Shark Interaction Tour: Available daily in Oslob, Cebu at 7:00 AM. Swim with the gentle giants in the clear waters of Oslob.
  • Bohol Countryside Tour: Departs from Tagbilaran City at 8:00 AM. Visit the Chocolate Hills, tarsier sanctuaries, and the Loboc River for a river cruise.
  • Manila City Historical Tour: Daily at 9:30 AM. Discover the historical highlights of the capital, including Intramuros, Rizal Park, and Fort Santiago.

Interesting Places to Visit

The Philippines is an archipelago of over 7,000 islands, with incredible diversity in landscapes and experiences. Here are some top places to visit:

Palawan

Known for its stunning limestone cliffs, hidden lagoons, and world-class beaches, Palawan is consistently ranked as one of the world’s best islands. Don’t miss El Nido, Coron, and the Underground River.

Chocolate Hills, Bohol

This natural wonder features over 1,200 conical hills that turn brown during the dry season, resembling chocolate mounds. It’s a unique geological formation that’s a must-see in Bohol.

Boracay

Famous for its powdery white sand beaches and vibrant nightlife, Boracay is a favorite destination for beach lovers. Experience water sports, sunset sailing, and lively beach parties.

Banaue Rice Terraces

Often called the "Eighth Wonder of the World," these 2,000-year-old rice terraces are a UNESCO World Heritage site, carved into the mountains by the Ifugao people. It's a breathtaking cultural and natural landscape.

Siargao

Known as the "Surfing Capital of the Philippines," Siargao boasts world-class waves, pristine lagoons, and a laid-back island vibe. It’s also a haven for nature lovers and adventurers.

Manila

The bustling capital city offers a mix of modern skyscrapers, colonial history, and vibrant street life. Visit historic sites like Intramuros, Rizal Park, and the National Museum for a deep dive into the country's culture.

Travel Tips

- Best Time to Visit: The best time to visit the Philippines is during the dry season from November to April. The rainy season typically runs from May to October, but this can vary by region.
- Currency: The local currency is the Philippine Peso (PHP). ATMs are widely available in cities and tourist areas, and credit cards are accepted in most hotels and restaurants.
- Getting Around: Domestic flights, ferries, and buses are common for inter-island travel. Jeepneys and tricycles are popular for getting around in towns and cities.
