Discover Thailand

A Land of Culture, Adventure, and Beaches

Transportation to Thailand

Thailand is easily accessible by air through its main international airports:

  • Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK) in Bangkok
  • Phuket International Airport (HKT) for southern islands
  • Chiang Mai International Airport (CNX) for the northern region
Thailand is also well-connected to neighboring countries via overland routes (buses and trains) from Laos, Cambodia, Malaysia, and Myanmar. Once in the country, domestic flights, trains, and buses provide excellent options for getting around.

Schedules for Popular Tours

There are plenty of popular tours that give you a taste of Thailand’s rich culture and stunning landscapes. Here are some top tours:

  • Bangkok Temple and Grand Palace Tour: Daily at 9:00 AM. Visit Wat Pho, Wat Arun, and the iconic Grand Palace.
  • Phi Phi Island Tour: Departs daily at 8:30 AM from Phuket or Krabi. Enjoy a full-day excursion to the stunning islands of Phi Phi.
  • Elephant Sanctuary Tour (Chiang Mai): Morning and afternoon tours available daily. Learn about elephant conservation and enjoy feeding and bathing these gentle giants.
  • Ayutthaya Historical Park Day Trip: Departs from Bangkok daily at 7:00 AM. Explore the ancient ruins of this UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Floating Market and Railway Market Tour: Daily departures from Bangkok at 7:00 AM. Experience Thailand’s vibrant market culture, both on land and water.

Interesting Places to Visit

Thailand is known for its beautiful temples, bustling markets, scenic islands, and natural wonders. Here are some top places to visit:

  • Bangkok: Thailand’s bustling capital is a mix of modern skyscrapers, ancient temples, and lively street markets. Must-visit spots include the Grand Palace, Wat Pho, and the riverside markets.
  • Phuket and Krabi: These southern provinces are famous for their white sandy beaches, clear waters, and iconic limestone cliffs. Explore nearby islands like Phi Phi, James Bond Island, and the Similan Islands.
  • Chiang Mai: Located in northern Thailand, Chiang Mai is known for its historic temples, night bazaars, and nearby elephant sanctuaries. Visit Doi Suthep Temple for panoramic views of the city.
  • Ayutthaya: The ancient capital of Thailand, Ayutthaya is filled with temple ruins, giant Buddha statues, and history. Take a day trip from Bangkok to explore this UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Railay Beach: Known for its towering limestone cliffs, Railay Beach in Krabi is a paradise for rock climbers and beach lovers alike.
  • Sukhothai Historical Park: Another UNESCO site, Sukhothai is home to the ruins of an ancient Thai kingdom, with temples and Buddha statues set against peaceful landscapes.

Travel Tips

- Best Time to Visit: The best time to visit Thailand is between November and February when the weather is cool and dry. The rainy season runs from May to October, though southern islands can still be enjoyable during this time.
- Currency: The local currency is the Thai Baht (THB). ATMs are widely available, and credit cards are accepted in most hotels, restaurants, and tourist shops.
- Getting Around: Thailand has a wide range of transportation options, including tuk-tuks, buses, and trains. Domestic flights are affordable and provide fast travel between regions. Long-tail boats and ferries are available for island-hopping adventures.
