Discover Thailand

A Land of Culture, Adventure, and Beaches

Transportation to Thailand

Thailand is accessible by several major international airports:

  • Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK) in Bangkok
  • Phuket International Airport (HKT) in Phuket
  • Chiang Mai International Airport (CNX) in Chiang Mai
Thailand is well-connected by overland routes from neighboring countries like Laos, Cambodia, Malaysia, and Myanmar. Once in Thailand, domestic flights, trains, and buses make it easy to travel across the country.

Schedules for Popular Tours

Thailand offers a wide range of tours that showcase its rich culture, stunning landscapes, and vibrant cities. Here are some popular options:

  • Bangkok Temple and Grand Palace Tour: Daily at 9:00 AM. Explore Wat Pho, Wat Arun, and the Grand Palace.
  • Phi Phi Island Day Trip: Departs daily at 8:00 AM from Phuket or Krabi. Enjoy crystal-clear waters, snorkeling, and island hopping.
  • Elephant Sanctuary Experience: Morning and afternoon tours available daily in Chiang Mai. Interact with and learn about rescued elephants in a responsible and ethical setting.
  • Ayutthaya Historical Park Day Trip: Departs daily from Bangkok at 7:30 AM. Discover the ancient ruins of this UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Floating Market and Railway Market Tour: Daily at 7:00 AM from Bangkok. Experience the bustling markets on water and the iconic railway market.

Interesting Places to Visit

Thailand is known for its stunning beaches, ancient temples, bustling cities, and lush landscapes. Here are some must-visit places:

Bangkok

Thailand’s bustling capital is a vibrant mix of modern skyscrapers, historic temples, and lively street markets. Must-see sites include the Grand Palace, Wat Pho, and Wat Arun, along with famous markets like Chatuchak and Damnoen Saduak.

Phuket

Known for its beautiful beaches, clear waters, and vibrant nightlife, Phuket is the perfect destination for beach lovers and adventure seekers. Explore Patong Beach, take a boat trip to nearby islands, and enjoy water sports.

Chiang Mai

The cultural hub of northern Thailand, Chiang Mai is known for its ancient temples, night bazaars, and mountainous landscapes. Visit Doi Suthep, the Old City, and explore nearby elephant sanctuaries and hill tribes.

Ayutthaya

Once the capital of the Kingdom of Siam, Ayutthaya is home to ancient temples and palaces that reflect its glorious past. This UNESCO World Heritage site is just a short trip from Bangkok.

Railay Beach

Accessible only by boat, Railay Beach in Krabi is famous for its towering limestone cliffs, white sands, and clear blue waters. It’s a paradise for rock climbers, beach-goers, and adventurers.

Sukhothai

Considered the birthplace of Thai culture, Sukhothai Historical Park contains the ruins of ancient temples and palaces. It's a UNESCO World Heritage site that provides insight into Thailand’s early history and architecture.

Travel Tips

- Best Time to Visit: The best time to visit Thailand is from November to February, when the weather is cooler and dry. The rainy season runs from May to October, but southern islands can still be enjoyable during this time.
- Currency: The local currency is the Thai Baht (THB). ATMs are widely available, and credit cards are accepted in most hotels, restaurants, and tourist spots.
- Getting Around: Thailand has excellent transportation options, including domestic flights, trains, buses, and tuk-tuks. Long-tail boats and ferries are available for island hopping.
