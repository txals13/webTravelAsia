Transportation to Vietnam

Vietnam is accessible by air through its major international airports:

  • Noi Bai International Airport (HAN) in Hanoi (North)
  • Tan Son Nhat International Airport (SGN) in Ho Chi Minh City (South)
  • Da Nang International Airport (DAD) in Central Vietnam
Vietnam Airlines and many international carriers offer frequent flights. If you're traveling from neighboring countries like Laos, Cambodia, or China, overland options by bus or train are also available.

Interesting Places to Visit

Vietnam offers a wide variety of destinations that cater to both nature lovers and culture enthusiasts. Here are some highlights:

  • Halong Bay: A UNESCO World Heritage site known for its emerald waters and thousands of limestone islands topped with rainforests. Ideal for boat cruises, kayaking, and exploring hidden caves.
  • Hanoi: The capital of Vietnam, known for its centuries-old architecture and rich culture. Must-see attractions include the Old Quarter, Hoan Kiem Lake, and the Temple of Literature.
  • Hoi An: A well-preserved ancient town offering a blend of Chinese, Japanese, and European influences. Stroll through colorful lantern-lit streets, enjoy traditional cuisine, and get custom-tailored clothing made.
  • Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park: Home to the world's largest caves, including the famous Son Doong Cave. You can trek, explore caves, and experience incredible underground rivers.
  • Mekong Delta: Known as the "Rice Bowl" of Vietnam, the Mekong Delta offers lush landscapes, floating markets, and a glimpse into rural Vietnamese life.
  • Sa Pa: Famous for its terraced rice fields and hiking opportunities, Sa Pa offers breathtaking views of the northern highlands and insight into the lives of Vietnam's ethnic minorities.

Travel Tips

- Best Time to Visit: The best time to visit northern Vietnam (Hanoi, Halong Bay, and Sapa) is from October to April, while the south (Ho Chi Minh City, Mekong Delta) is great from November to March. Central Vietnam (Da Nang, Hoi An) is ideal from February to August.
- Currency: The local currency is the Vietnamese Dong (VND). Most places accept cash, but credit cards are becoming increasingly accepted in major cities.
- Getting Around: Vietnam's public transportation includes buses, trains, and domestic flights. Grab (ride-hailing) and motorbike taxis are common and affordable for shorter distances.
